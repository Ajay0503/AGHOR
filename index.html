Manufacturing

Manufacturing clothing and accessories for AGHOR typically involves several stages, including designing, sourcing materials, pattern making, cutting, sewing, finishing, and quality control.
During the design phase, AGHOR will create sketches or computerized images of the products they want to produce, including the style, color, and materials.
Once the design is finalized, AGHOR will source the necessary materials from suppliers. This could include fabric, buttons, zippers, and other components.
Pattern making is the process of creating a blueprint for the garment or accessory that will guide the cutting and sewing processes.
Next, the materials are cut into the necessary shapes and sizes according to the pattern. This can be done using manual or automated cutting machines.
The sewing process involves stitching the cut pieces together to create the final product. Different machines and techniques are used for different types of stitches and seams.
After the garment or accessory is assembled, it undergoes finishing, which includes tasks such as hemming, adding buttons or zippers, and adding labels or tags.
Finally, the products go through a quality control process to ensure they meet the AGHOR's standards for fit, durability, and overall quality
